EDUCATION

CREDIT
UNION

  MICRO-LENDING                          

During my voluntary teaching period at An Giang University, I learned that most of the students come from rural areas in the province, many of whom come from remote or economically disadvantaged families. They have experienced years of hardship and struggle to make it to the university. Due to cultural factors, agricultural needs, and economic difficulties in their families, many rural children are forced to drop out of primary school. Some of my outstanding students have persevered under great pressure, relying on their own unyielding efforts to win scholarships from the school and government to complete their studies. Although the scholarships are meager, they still manage to save some of that money to help with their families. Their perseverance is truly admirable and touching. While I have provided some assistance, establishing a credit union to serve the education sector can provide more sustainable and extensive support.

TEACHERS
CO-OP

Although An Giang University is affiliated with the National University of Ho Chi Minh City, which is directly under the Prime Minister's Office, newly recruited teachers, even those with a Ph.D., only receive a salary of about $200, which is far from sufficient for their living expenses. Therefore, most teachers must seek additional income outside of their formal teaching positions.

In Long Xuyen City, the capital of An Giang Province where the University is located, private language centers typically only pay their teachers 30% of the tuition fees collected. Several of my outstanding students graduated last year and have become teachers themselves. However, despite their new careers, they still face economic and job pressures, and life is not easy for them. I suggest that they refer to the models of the UK and the US and explore the possibility of establishing an educational cooperative, where teachers can receive 70% of tuition fees, which can significantly improve the current situation.
